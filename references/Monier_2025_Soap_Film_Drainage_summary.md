## Key Concepts

### Marginal Regeneration
- **Definition**: Continuous replacement of thick film regions by thin film elements (TFEs) formed at the meniscus
- Primary mechanism governing soap film drainage
- Robust across wide range of gravitational conditions (0.2g to 100g)

### Thick Film Elements (TFEs)
- Thin regions that propagate from meniscus into film
- Thickness ratio between TFEs and adjacent film: **0.8 - 0.9**
- Consistent across all gravity conditions tested
- Motion exhibits inertia-to-viscous transition

### Capillary Suction
- Drives drainage at film-meniscus interface
- Rate controlled by meniscus size
- Gravity affects amplitude through meniscus geometry

### Thin-Film Interferometry
- Experimental technique for time-resolved thickness maps
- Enables quantitative measurement of drainage dynamics
- Sub-micron thickness resolution

## Equations/Formulas

### Drainage Scaling Laws
