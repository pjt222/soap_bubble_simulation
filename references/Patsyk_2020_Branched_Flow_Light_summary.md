## Key Concepts

### Branched Flow
- **Definition**: When waves propagate through weak disordered potential with correlation length larger than wavelength, they form channels (branches) of enhanced intensity
- Branches keep dividing as waves propagate
- Fundamental wave phenomenon previously observed in electrons, sound, tsunamis

### Soap Film as Optical Medium
- Thin soap membranes have smooth thickness variations
- These variations act as a **correlated disordered potential**
- Light focuses into filaments displaying branched flow features
- Film thickness variation → refractive index variation → optical path differences

### Experimental Observation
- First direct observation of branched flow of light
- Light propagates through soap film, forming visible branching patterns
- Filaments maintain collimation despite medium randomness
- Counter-intuitive: linear mechanism produces focused channels

## Physics Background

### Correlation Length
For branched flow to occur:
$$l_c > \lambda$$
where $l_c$ is correlation length of disorder and $\lambda$ is wavelength.

### Optical Path Difference
Through film of thickness $d$ and refractive index $n$:
$$\Delta = 2nd\cos\theta$$

### Random Caustics
Branching occurs at random caustic formation:
- Weak fluctuations in medium properties
- Focus energy into narrow channels
- Universal phenomenon across wave types
