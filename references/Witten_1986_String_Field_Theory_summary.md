## Witten (1986) - Non-commutative Geometry and String Field Theory

**Citation:** Witten, E. (1986). Non-commutative geometry and string field theory. *Nuclear Physics B*, 268(2), 253-294.

**DOI:** 10.1016/0550-3213(86)90155-0

**Funding:** NSF Grant PHY-80-19754

## Abstract

An attempt is made to interpret the interactions of bosonic open strings as defining a non-commutative, associative algebra, and to formulate the classical non-linear field theory of such strings in the language of non-commutative geometry. The point of departure is the BRST approach to string field theory. A setting is given in which there is a unique gauge invariant action, whose linearized approximation reproduces the conventional Veneziano spectrum. A derivation of conventional Veneziano model amplitudes from this gauge invariant action is sketched. Some brief comments are made about attempts to extend these results to open superstrings and to closed strings.

## Key Concepts

### String Field Theory
- **Goal**: Formulate string theory as a field theory
- Fields are functionals on the space of string configurations
- Enables systematic treatment of string interactions

### Non-commutative Geometry
- Generalization of geometry where coordinates don't commute
- $[x^i, x^j] \neq 0$
- String interactions naturally lead to non-commutative structures

### BRST Formalism
- **BRST**: Becchi-Rouet-Stora-Tyutin
- Systematic approach to gauge symmetries
- Nilpotent BRST operator $Q$ with $Q^2 = 0$
- Physical states: $Q|\psi\rangle = 0$

### Witten's Star Product
String field interaction via associative product:
$$\Psi_1 * \Psi_2$$
- Glues strings at midpoint
- Associative: $(\Psi_1 * \Psi_2) * \Psi_3 = \Psi_1 * (\Psi_2 * \Psi_3)$
- Non-commutative: $\Psi_1 * \Psi_2 \neq \Psi_2 * \Psi_1$

## The Action

### Cubic String Field Theory Action
$$S = -\frac{1}{2}\langle\Psi, Q\Psi\rangle - \frac{g}{3}\langle\Psi, \Psi * \Psi\rangle$$

where:
- $\Psi$ is the string field
- $Q$ is the BRST operator
- $g$ is the coupling constant
- $\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle$ is the BPZ inner product

### Gauge Invariance
Invariant under:
$$\delta\Psi = Q\Lambda + g(\Psi * \Lambda - \Lambda * \Psi)$$

## Key Results

1. **Uniqueness**: Gauge invariant action is unique (up to field redefinitions)
2. **Linearization**: Reproduces Veneziano spectrum at low energy
